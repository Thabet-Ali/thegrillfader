# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Private event hosts: people planning a wedding, milestone birthday, anniversary, or
private gathering who want fire-built BBQ catering with a sense of occasion, not a
buffet line. They arrive curious, often from word of mouth or the TV4 *Grillmästarna*
association, on a phone, evaluating whether this one pitmaster is the real thing and
worth booking. The single job the site exists to get done: move that visitor to a
**submitted booking inquiry**. Everything else (origin story, menu, credibility) serves
that one conversion. Audience is bilingual (Swedish primary, English supported).

## Product Purpose

A single-page, single-file marketing site for The Grillfader: fire-built BBQ catering
run by one pitmaster, born on TV4's *Grillmästarna* in 2015. It exists to convert a
curious visitor into a booking inquiry by making the brand feel like a singular,
cinematic experience rather than a catering vendor. Success = inquiries submitted; the
site wins when a visitor finishes it believing this is the one person they want running
their event. The Godfather conceit ("an offer you can't refuse") is the spine: authority,
intimacy, and a touch of theater.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, authoritative, intimate. Three words: **cinematic, singular, unhurried.**
The voice is first-person and confident, the way a craftsman who answers to no one talks
("I don't cater parties. I run them."), with a dry Godfather wink that never tips into
costume. It should evoke the hush before a film's opening scene: low light, fire, a
single figure who clearly knows exactly what he's doing. Restraint is the luxury signal,
not ornament. Warmth comes from fire, brass, and the human first-person voice, never from
softness or friendliness.

## Anti-references

- **Generic catering / restaurant template.** No stock-photo grids, no "Welcome to our
  restaurant," no soft pastel friendliness, no smiling-staff hero.
- **Rustic-farmhouse BBQ joint.** No chalkboard fonts, kraft-paper textures, distressed
  wood, mason jars, or "smokehouse" clichés. This is luxury, not down-home.
- **Loud SaaS / startup landing.** No gradient blobs, glassmorphism, tiny tracked
  eyebrows over every section, hero-metric templates, or identical feature-card grids.
- **Cluttered / over-decorated.** Nothing that fights the cinematic restraint. When a
  section feels busy, the fix is to remove, not to polish. One idea per screen.

## Design Principles

1. **Restraint is the luxury.** Simpler, bolder, fewer. When something feels off, the
   move is to remove an element and enlarge what stays, never to add polish or decoration.
2. **One pitmaster, first person.** The whole site speaks as one man. Copy stays in his
   voice; the design never dilutes that into committee-brand neutrality.
3. **Cinematic, not decorative.** Fire, shadow, and motion set a scene with intent; they
   are direction, not garnish. Every effect must earn its place in the mood.
4. **Every screen points at the booking.** Each section moves the visitor one step closer
   to the inquiry; nothing is there purely to look impressive.
5. **Show the craft, don't claim it.** Authority is demonstrated (the work, the origin,
   the menu), not asserted with adjectives.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target; best-effort with reasonable defaults. Practical baseline given the
brand is built on motion and low-light contrast: keep body and interactive text legible
against the dark surface, and honor `prefers-reduced-motion` with a calm fallback so the
cinematic motion never traps anyone. Bilingual EN/SV is a first-class concern: copy and
layout must hold at both languages' lengths.
